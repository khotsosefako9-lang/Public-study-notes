Ch 5 — Welfare Theory, Voting & Rent Seeking
Pareto & Nozick NB
Pareto: ≥1 better off, nobody worse off. Rarely met. 3 compatible justifications for redistribution: (1) Externality — helping poor benefits all; (2) Insurance motive — veil of ignorance → choose social insurance; (3) Altruism — rich derive utility from poor's welfare
Nozick (libertarian): Redistribute only to fix injustice
Acquisition: May acquire unowned things without worsening others
Transfer: Voluntary transfers of justly held goods are just
Rectification: Unjust acquisition/transfer must be corrected
Welfare Functions & Rawls
Cardinal (Utilitarian)
Ordinal
W = U_A + U_B — utility measurable & comparable
W = W(U_A,U_B) — rankings only; no comparison needed
Rawls maximin: W = Min(U_A, U_B) — W = lowest utility. To raise W, must raise the worst-off. Veil of ignorance → rational agents choose this. Limitation: ignores everyone else; may justify huge sacrifices for tiny gain to poorest
Optimal welfare: point on utility possibility curve (QR) tangent to highest W contour = point H
Voting Rules & Median Voter NB
Rule
Key point
Dictatorial
1 person decides all
Unanimity (100%)
All agree; protects all; prone to veto
Majority (50%+1)
Most common; faster; no minority veto
Plurality
Highest count wins; no majority needed
Median voter theorem: Middle voter's preferred outcome always wins under majority rule. Any deviation can be beaten by a proposal closer to the median. Assumes rational, single-peaked preferences
Decision Costs & Arrow NB Graph
C_D (decision)Slopes UP — harder to agree as threshold rises
C_E (external)Slopes DOWN — fewer outvoted as threshold rises
C_T = C_D+C_EU-shaped. Min at M* = optimal majority (>50%, <100%)
Arrow's impossibility: No rule satisfies all 5 at once: (1) Rationality (2) Transitivity — X>Y, Y>Z → X>Z, else Condorcet paradox (3) Non-dictatorship (4) Pareto — unanimous preference respected (5) IIA — X vs Y ranking unaffected by Z

Tax Neutrality & Lump Sum NB
Neutral tax: No excess burden — welfare loss = revenue exactly. General taxes approach neutrality. Selective taxes are non-neutral.
Lump sum: Fixed amount regardless of behaviour → no substitution effect → no DWL → Pareto-efficient. Limitation: regressive; ignores ability to pay; impractical
